Topic for discussion

A place to sleep

A tem of people made a special sleeping pod for homeless people. They are testing it in the city of Ulm, Germany.

It is necessary to check if the pod is warm enough to keep homeless people safe from cold and wind. If the test is successful, the team wants to put the pods in other German cities, too. The pod has a solar panel on the roof which makes light and it also opens the door. The pod is from metal and wood and it is suitable for one person. The door unlocks in the evening and someone, who has no place to sleep, can stay inside. The pod is warm and there is fresh air inside.

When the door opens, social workers get a message. They check and clean the place the next day to make sure that it is ready for another person.

Source: A Place to Sleep - level 2 | News in Levels

Vocabularies:

  • sleeping pod: hộp ngủ
  • homeless people: người vô gia cư
  • solar panel: tấm năng lượng mặt trời
  • metal and wood: kim loại và gỗ

Questions for discussion

  1. What is the story about?
  2. What did German do for homeless people?
  3. Can you describe a homeless person?
  4. Why do we see a lot homeless people nowadays? What make them homeless?
  5. Can you imagine how homeless people live? How can they earn a living?
  6. Do we see any support from community to homeless people? What are they?
  7. If you are the president of the city where many homeless people are found, do you want to build houses or pods for homeless people?
Let's learn and apply

3 phrasal verbs for feelings

1/ To bottle something up means to not express your feelings. It usually relates a negative feeling that can get worse by not expressing it. This phrasal verb is separable and needs an object. Example:

  • She bottled up her feelings - it ended up making her feel unwell.
  • She bottled her feelings up - it ended up making her feel unwell.

2/ To take something out on someone means to make someone suffer, even when it's not their fault. This phrasal verb is separable and needs an object. Example:

  • He was having a bad day and took his frustration out on me.
  • He was having a bad day and took out his frustration on me.

3/ To let someone down means to disappoint someone. This phrasal verb is separable and needs an object. Example:

  • She let her boss down when she forgot to go to the meeting.
  • She let down her boss when she forgot to go to the meeting.
